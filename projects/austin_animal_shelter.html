Shelter App

The Project

Shelter App was created to be a rapid prototype of what a native mobile app would look like and how it would be used. Two other designers and I made this app and prototyped it in a weekend. We designed all the screens and user flows from scratch, then used InVision to prototype the app's functionality.

The Tools

• Design Research
• UX Research
• Sketches, Wireframes, Mockups
• Photoshop
• Illustrator
• Invision Prototype

Research

Problem:

People who volunteer to foster animals from shelter’s often have jobs and other things that keep them busy. Most times, these foster parents can’t get out of certain obligations to take their pet in to the clinic for appointments or to various adoption events on weekends. However other volunteers working at the shelter are available take these pets to different appointments, but unfortunately don’t have time to foster a pet full time. At the moment the shelter has to act as a middleman to connect foster parents with volunteers who can help out from time to time.

The Solution:

The other designers and I put our heads together and came up with an idea for an app that is designed to effortlessly connect foster parents and volunteers for pet rides. We implemented an Uber-like system of ride requests to streamline and simplify the ride-giving process, and we found a way to make it more exciting for all of the parties involved.
