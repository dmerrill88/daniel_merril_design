Goodreads Redesign

The Project

Goodreads is a popular social web app owned by Amazon for people that enjoy reading books. My task was to responsively redesign the site to be more organized, intuitive, and user friendly. I was allotted 2 days to do this project from start to finish.

The Tools

• Design Research
• UX Research
• User Personas
• User Senarios
• Sketches
• Mockups (Illustrator)

The Research

Since doing my redesign of Goodreads they have redesigned their site themselves. Although it’s much better now it was a disaster when I decided to take on the project. My first experience with good reads wasn’t a great one. The colors, typography, and navigation of the site were all a problem. After signing into the site I started exploring. I added books I had read, rated books, used my cellphone, and created bookshelves.

I realized that the site is very helpful. It keeps track of books I'm reading, helps me quickly scan lists of books I have read or want to read, and shows me what my friends are currently reading as well. However navigating through the site was anything but intuitive. From a usability standpoint, the current Goodreads site is awful. Even after days of carefully using the site, I still found myself confused or lost. Features are duplicated and overcomplicated, and a general lack of hierarchy makes every conceivable action equally important.

User Personas and Senarios

I created three user personas and senarios. Each representing a different type of GoodReads' user, and all users having a unique set of values and goals.

Style Tiles

For the project, I wanted to create three totally different styles to see what range of feelings I could get from each. I ended up taking ideas from each of the tiles and combined them to create one ultimate mockup. I took the colors from style tile 1, the design patterns from stile tile 2, and the typography of stile tile 3.
